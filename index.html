Night Messenger · Evolutionary Miracle

Owls have unique feather structures and extraordinary sensory systems. Their eyes are tubular and cannot rotate, thus evolving an astonishing 270 degree neck flexibility.
Most owls are nocturnal and rely on their sharp hearing and almost silent wings to hunt. From the Arctic snowy owl to the Southeast Asian woodpecker, they have adapted to diverse habitats such as forests, grasslands, and deserts.
